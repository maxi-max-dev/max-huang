01What I've built & run

AI as staff, not novelty

Each of these runs on its own. I designed the roles, the routing, the failure handling — and I have no engineering background.

MiroFish — a prediction engine that writes a morning brief

A multi-agent “swarm simulation”: it ingests the day's news and policy seeds, builds a small world of AI personas, runs them forward, and writes a structured forecast with a confidence rating.

Pipeline

News seed → knowledge graph + persona generation → multi-round agent simulation → report synthesis → delivery.

How I used AI

Qwen LLMs drive the agents; Zep gives them long-term memory; the OASIS framework runs the simulation. I wired the pipeline and the daily trigger end-to-end.

Result

Runs unattended every morning and posts the brief to a channel — no hands on it. A recent run forecast the US–China tariff talks at medium-high confidence.

OpenClaw — a 5-agent system I run 24/7

My personal operations desk: five specialised agents — an orchestrator, a market-trading agent, a news monitor, a research/judgment agent, and a mobile agent — that research, draft, trade (in a sharemarket game), and report on their own.

Scope

~A dozen scheduled jobs running live: morning briefs, weekly news summaries, market reviews, job-market scans, nightly self-review.

How I used AI

I authored each agent's role, model routing, tool permissions and failure handling, plus the schedules — and added live-data browser automation and semantic memory.

Result

A working model of “AI as back-office staff”: it runs unattended with failure alerts and self-recovery when something drops.
